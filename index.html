Mitchell Piehl

Student, Computer Scientist, AI/ML researcher

Mitchell Piehl is a computer science student and avid AI researcher graduating from the University of St. Thomas in May. While completing his degree in computer science, he also got minors in data science, philosophy and science, and logic and analytical reasoning. After graduation, he will attend the University of Iowa his PhD in computer science, focusing his research on Artificial Intelligence and Natural Language Processing.

Mitchell has skills and backgrounds ranging from software engineering and web development to AI development and optimization, in both research and production settings. He also has a deep background in philosophy and science that helps him to understand the ethical implications of his past and future work in artificial intelligence.

Outside of academia, Mitchell is also on the track and field team at St. Thomas. On the track and field team he is a sprinter with six St. Thomas school records (200m, 300m, 400m, 4x100m, 4x400m, and DMR). Mitchell also serves as team captain and represents the track team as the Student-Athlete Advisory Committee representative.
